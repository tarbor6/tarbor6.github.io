Add the right and left vagus nerves.

The right and left vagus nerves follow the espohagus thru the superior and posterior mediastinum.
In the drawing, note the cardiac sympathetic trunk, which follows the path of the vagus and moves anteriorly at the tracheobronchial bifrucation with the cardiac branches of the vagal nerve.

These nerves are responsible for the autonomic control of the heart and regulate heart rate, force of contraction and cardiac output.

Unfortunately, these nerves are too small to be seen on our cadaver.
Add the ligamentum arteriosum which is the remnent of the ductus arteriosis.
Add the right and left recurrent layngeal nerves.

Note that the left recurrent laryngeal nerve wraps under the aorta very near to the ligamentum arteriosum.

The right recurrent goes under the right subclavian artery.
