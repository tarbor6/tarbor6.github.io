What structures pass through the transverse foramen (foramen transversarium)?

The vertebral artery, vertebral vein, and sympathetic nerve fibers.

All cervical verebrae have a transverse foramen, but the contents are distinctive in one of them. Which one?

The vertebra prominens, or seventh cervical vertebra (C7). In the C7 vertebra, the transverse foramen typically only transmits the vertebral vein and sympathetic fibers. The vertebral arteries usually enters the cervical transverse foramina of C6.
