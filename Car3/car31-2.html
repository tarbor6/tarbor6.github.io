What nerves innervate the parietal pleura and how is this important in assessing pain during breathing?

The parietal pleura is innervated by somatic afferents from the intercostal nerves and therefore pain would be felt as thoracic wall pain. This is different from the diaphragmatic and mediastinal somatic afferents which orginate from the phrenic nerve (C2-C4) and therefore pain is felt in the laternal neck and supraclavicular regions.
